## Overview

Collections in Lynx Note help you organize related notes into groups, making it easier to find and manage your content. Collections can contain multiple notes and can be shared with friends for collaborative organization.

## What Are Collections?

Collections are organizational containers that:
- Group related notes together
- Can be shared with friends
- Display the number of notes they contain
- Show both your own notes and notes shared with you
- Help maintain a structured note-taking system

<Tip>
  Think of collections as folders or categories for your notes. Use them to group notes by project, topic, time period, or any other organizational scheme that works for you.
</Tip>

## Creating a Collection

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Collection Creation">
    From the collections page, click "New Collection" or "Create Collection" to open the creation form.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Name Your Collection">
    Enter a descriptive name that clearly identifies the collection's purpose. Examples:
    - "Work Projects"
    - "Personal Goals 2024"
    - "Recipe Collection"
    - "Meeting Notes"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save the Collection">
    Click "Create Collection" to save. You'll be redirected to the collections page with a confirmation message.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Collections start empty and are automatically associated with your user account. You can add notes to them during note creation or editing.
</Note>

## Viewing Collections

Your collections page displays all accessible collections:

### Your Collections
- Collections you've created
- Full management permissions
- Can add/remove notes
- Can share with friends
- Can rename or delete

### Shared Collections
- Collections shared with you by friends
- Can view and edit
- Can add your notes to them
- Cannot delete (only the owner can)
- Marked with a special indicator

### Collection Display

Collections are sorted by the number of notes they contain (largest first), making your most-used collections easy to find. Each collection shows:
- Collection name
- Number of notes inside
- Sharing status (shared or not)
- Owner information for shared collections

## Adding Notes to Collections

There are two ways to add notes to collections:

### During Note Creation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Your Note">
    While creating a new note, look for the "Collections" section in the note editor.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Collections">
    Choose one or more collections from the available list. This includes:
    - Collections you own
    - Collections shared with you
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save the Note">
    When you save the note, it's automatically added to the selected collections.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### During Note Editing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Note">
    Navigate to an existing note and click "Edit".
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Update Collections">
    The editor shows which collections currently contain this note. You can:
    - Add the note to new collections
    - Remove it from existing collections
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save Changes">
    Save the note to update its collection assignments.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  A single note can belong to multiple collections. Use this feature to create overlapping organizational schemes - for example, a note could be in both "Work Projects" and "Q1 Goals".
</Tip>

## Viewing Collection Contents

### Your Own Collections

When you view a collection you own from the main collections page, the notes are displayed inline, making it easy to see what's inside at a glance.

### Shared Collections

Collections shared with you have a dedicated detail view:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Shared Collection">
    Click on a collection that's been shared with you from the collections page.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View All Notes">
    See all notes within the collection, including:
    - Notes owned by the collection owner
    - Notes added by other users with access
    - Full note details and content
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Interact with Notes">
    You can view, edit, and manage notes within shared collections according to your permissions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  When viewing your own collections, only notes you've created are displayed. When viewing shared collections, you see all notes added by any user with access.
</Note>

## Editing Collections

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the Collection">
    Navigate to the collection you want to edit. You can only edit collections you created.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Make Changes">
    Click the "Edit" button to modify the collection name or other properties.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save Updates">
    Save your changes. The behavior after saving depends on the sharing status:
    - For your own collections: Redirected to the collections page
    - For shared collections you're viewing: Returned to the shared collection detail view
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The notes within a collection are managed separately. Editing a collection only changes its properties, not its contents. To modify which notes are in a collection, edit the individual notes.
</Note>

## Sharing Collections

Share entire collections with friends to collaborate on organized note groups:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Initiate Sharing">
    From a collection you own, click the "Share" button to open the sharing interface.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Friends">
    Choose which friends should have access to the collection. You can only share with accepted friends.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm Sharing">
    Save the sharing settings. Your selected friends will:
    - Receive a notification about the shared collection
    - See the collection in their collections page
    - Be able to view all notes within it
    - Be able to add their own notes to it
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Sharing a collection is perfect for team projects, study groups, or any collaborative effort where multiple people need to organize notes together.
</Tip>

### Managing Shared Access

After sharing a collection:
- Add more friends at any time
- Remove friends who no longer need access
- Friends receive notifications when their access changes
- You maintain full control as the collection owner

See the [Collaborating guide](/guides/collaborating) for more details on sharing and notifications.

## Deleting Collections

When you no longer need a collection:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the Collection">
    Navigate to the collection you want to delete. You can only delete collections you created.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm Deletion">
    Click the "Delete" button and confirm your decision.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Notes Remain Safe">
    The collection is removed, but:
    - All notes within it are preserved
    - Notes remain in your notes list
    - Notes are only removed from this collection, not deleted
    - Shared users lose access to the collection
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Deleting a collection only removes the organizational container. Your notes remain safe and accessible from your notes dashboard and any other collections they belong to.
</Note>

## Collections vs. Notes: Understanding the Relationship

Collections and notes have a flexible many-to-many relationship:

- **One note, multiple collections**: A note can belong to several collections simultaneously
- **One collection, multiple notes**: A collection can contain numerous notes
- **Independent lifecycle**: Deleting a note removes it from all collections, but deleting a collection doesn't delete the notes
- **Flexible organization**: Reorganize notes across collections without losing data

## Best Practices

### Naming Conventions

<Tip>
  Use clear, descriptive names for collections. Consider including context like dates, project names, or categories: "Client Work 2024", "Personal - Health", or "Team Meeting Notes".
</Tip>

### Organization Strategies

**By Project**
- Create collections for each major project
- Add all related notes as you work
- Share with team members for collaboration

**By Time Period**
- Organize notes by month, quarter, or year
- Easy to archive older collections
- Great for periodic reviews

**By Topic**
- Group notes by subject matter
- Useful for research or study notes
- Can overlap with project-based collections

**By Status**
- "Active Projects", "Completed", "On Hold"
- Move notes between collections as status changes
- Helps with workflow management

### Maintenance Tips

- **Review regularly**: Periodically check collections and remove outdated assignments
- **Avoid over-organization**: Too many collections can be as confusing as too few
- **Use sharing wisely**: Only share collections with people who truly need access
- **Clean up empty collections**: Delete collections that no longer serve a purpose
- **Consistent structure**: Develop a naming and organization scheme that works for you

## Permissions and Access

### As Collection Owner
- Full control over the collection
- Can rename or delete at any time
- Can share with any accepted friends
- Can modify sharing settings
- See all notes added by shared users

### As Shared User
- Can view all notes in the collection
- Can add your notes to the collection
- Can edit notes within the collection (following note permissions)
- Cannot rename or delete the collection
- Cannot modify sharing settings

### Access Restrictions
- Cannot access collections you don't own or haven't been shared
- Attempting to access restricted collections redirects you to the collections page
