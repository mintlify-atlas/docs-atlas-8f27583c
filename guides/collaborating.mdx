## Overview

Lynx Note's collaboration features enable you to work with friends, share content, and stay informed about shared activities. This guide covers friendships, sharing, and notifications to help you collaborate effectively.

## Managing Friendships

Before you can share notes and collections, you need to connect with other Lynx Note users through friendships.

### Understanding Friendship Status

Friendships in Lynx Note have two states:

- **Pending**: A friend request has been sent but not yet accepted
- **Accepted**: Both users have confirmed the friendship and can now share content

### Sending Friend Requests

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Friends Page">
    Navigate to the Friends or Friendships section from the main menu.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add a Friend">
    Click "Add Friend" or "New Friendship" to see available users.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select a User">
    Choose someone from the list of available users. The list automatically excludes:
    - Yourself
    - Users you've already sent requests to
    - Users who've sent you requests
    - Existing friends
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Send Request">
    Confirm your selection. The request is sent immediately, and the recipient receives a notification.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  When you send a friend request, the recipient gets a notification with your name, letting them know you'd like to connect.
</Note>

### Accepting Friend Requests

When someone sends you a friend request:

<Steps>
  <Step title="View Pending Requests">
    Check your Friends page for incoming friend requests. These appear in a dedicated "Friend Requests" section.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review the Request">
    See who sent the request and decide whether to accept.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Accept or Decline">
    - **Accept**: Click "Accept" to establish the friendship. The sender receives a notification that you've accepted.
    - **Decline**: Remove the request. The sender receives a notification that their request was declined.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Both accepting and declining friend requests send notifications to the requester, keeping everyone informed about the status of their connection attempts.
</Tip>

### Viewing Your Friends

Your Friends page shows three categories:

**Friend Requests** (Incoming)
- Pending requests from others waiting for your response
- Shows the requester's name and profile

**Pending Requests** (Outgoing)
- Friend requests you've sent that haven't been accepted yet
- Shows the recipient's name

**Friends** (Accepted)
- All established friendships
- Shows shared content for each friend:
  - Notes they've shared with you
  - Collections they've shared with you
  - Easy access to all shared content

### Removing Friendships

If you need to end a friendship:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Locate the Friendship">
    Find the friend in your accepted friends list or pending requests.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Delete the Friendship">
    Click the delete or remove button and confirm your decision.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Automatic Notifications">
    The other user receives a notification about the friendship removal. The message varies:
    - For pending requests: "[Your name] has declined your request"
    - For accepted friendships: "[Your name] has deleted your friendship"
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Removing a friendship doesn't automatically unshare content. However, you won't be able to share new content with that user unless you re-establish the friendship.
</Note>

## Sharing Notes

Share individual notes with friends for collaboration:

### Creating a Share

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Note">
    Navigate to a note you own and want to share.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Initiate Sharing">
    Click the "Share" button to open the sharing interface.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Friends">
    Choose one or more accepted friends to share with. You can only share with users who have accepted your friendship.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm Sharing">
    Save your selections. Each selected friend:
    - Receives a notification: "[Your name] has shared a note with you"
    - Can now view and edit the note
    - Sees the note in their notes dashboard
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  You must select at least one friend when sharing. The system won't let you create an empty share - if no one is selected, you'll see an error asking you to choose at least one friend.
</Tip>

### Managing Shared Access

After sharing a note, you can modify who has access:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Sharing Settings">
    Open the note and click "Edit Sharing" or manage the existing share.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Modify Friends List">
    Add or remove friends from the share:
    - **Adding friends**: New friends receive notifications and immediate access
    - **Removing friends**: Removed friends lose access and receive a notification: "[Your name] has deleted a note with you"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save Changes">
    Confirm your modifications. Notifications are sent automatically to affected users.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  You cannot remove all friends from a shared note. At least one friend must have access, or you need to delete the share entirely.
</Note>

### Unsharing Notes

To completely stop sharing a note:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access the Note">
    Open the note you want to unshare.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Delete the Share">
    Click "Unshare" or delete the sharing relationship.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Automatic Notifications">
    All friends who had access receive a notification: "[Your name] has deleted a note with you"
    The note becomes private again and is removed from their dashboards.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Leaving a Shared Note

If someone shared a note with you and you no longer want access:

1. Find the note in your shared content
2. Click "Leave" or "Remove from my notes"
3. You'll immediately lose access to the note
4. The note owner isn't notified of your departure

## Sharing Collections

Share entire collections for collaborative organization:

### Creating a Collection Share

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the Collection">
    Navigate to a collection you own that you want to share.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Open Sharing Interface">
    Click the "Share" button on the collection.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose Friends">
    Select one or more accepted friends to share with.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm Sharing">
    Save your selections. Each friend:
    - Receives a notification: "[Your name] has shared a collection with you"
    - Can view all notes in the collection
    - Can add their own notes to the collection
    - Sees the collection in their collections page
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Managing Collection Shares

Modify who has access to your shared collections:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Edit Sharing Settings">
    Open the collection and access the sharing management interface.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Update Friends List">
    - **Add friends**: They receive notifications and immediate access
    - **Remove friends**: They lose access and receive a notification: "[Your name] has deleted a collection with you"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save Changes">
    Confirm modifications. All changes trigger appropriate notifications.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Similar to notes, you cannot remove all friends from a shared collection. At least one friend must have access, or you need to delete the share entirely.
</Note>

### Unsharing Collections

To stop sharing a collection completely:

1. Open the collection
2. Access sharing settings
3. Click "Unshare" or delete the sharing relationship
4. All shared friends receive notifications and lose access
5. The collection becomes private again

### Leaving a Shared Collection

If you have access to a collection shared by someone else and want to leave:

1. Navigate to the shared collection
2. Click "Leave" or "Remove from my collections"
3. You immediately lose access
4. Any notes you added remain in the collection

## Notifications

Stay informed about collaboration activities with Lynx Note's notification system.

### Types of Notifications

**Friend Requests**
- When someone sends you a friend request
- When someone accepts your friend request
- When someone declines your friend request
- When someone removes your friendship

**Note Sharing**
- When someone shares a note with you
- When someone removes a note they shared with you
- When sharing access to a note changes

**Collection Sharing**
- When someone shares a collection with you
- When someone removes a collection they shared with you
- When sharing access to a collection changes

### Accessing Notifications

Notifications are available through:
- A notifications icon or bell in the main navigation
- A dedicated notifications page
- Real-time updates as events occur

### Managing Notifications

<Steps>
  <Step title="View Notifications">
    Click the notifications icon to see all recent notifications.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Read Notifications">
    Each notification includes:
    - The user who triggered the event
    - The type of event (friend request, share, etc.)
    - When it occurred
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Dismiss Notifications">
    Delete notifications individually to keep your notification list clean and focused on recent activity.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Check your notifications regularly to stay updated on sharing changes, friend requests, and collaboration activities. This helps you respond promptly and maintain effective collaboration.
</Tip>

### Notification Cleanup

The system automatically manages certain notifications:
- When a new friend request is sent to you, old friend request notifications from that user are deleted
- When you accept a friend request, the notification is updated
- This prevents notification clutter and ensures you see the most current information

## Collaboration Permissions

### For Shared Notes

**As Note Owner**
- Full control over the note
- Can edit all content
- Can manage who has access
- Can unshare or delete at any time

**As Shared User**
- Can view the complete note
- Can edit content (text, lists, files)
- Cannot delete the note
- Cannot manage sharing settings
- Can leave the share voluntarily

### For Shared Collections

**As Collection Owner**
- Full control over the collection
- Can rename or delete it
- Can manage sharing settings
- See all notes added by any user

**As Shared User**
- Can view all notes in the collection
- Can add your own notes to it
- Can edit notes according to note permissions
- Cannot delete the collection
- Cannot modify sharing settings
- Can leave the collection

## Best Practices for Collaboration

### Communication

<Tip>
  While Lynx Note handles the technical aspects of sharing, maintain good communication with your collaborators about how you're using shared notes and collections.
</Tip>

### Organization

- **Clear naming**: Use descriptive names for shared content so everyone understands the purpose
- **Consistent structure**: Agree on organizational schemes with frequent collaborators
- **Regular cleanup**: Remove access for users who no longer need it

### Security

- **Share selectively**: Only share with people who truly need access
- **Review regularly**: Periodically check who has access to your shared content
- **Remove promptly**: When a collaboration ends, remove sharing access to protect your content

### Etiquette

- **Respect shared content**: Don't delete or drastically modify shared notes without discussion
- **Communicate changes**: Let collaborators know about major updates to shared content
- **Leave gracefully**: If you no longer need access to shared content, leave the share to reduce clutter

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

**Can't share with someone**
- Ensure you have an accepted friendship
- Check that the friendship isn't pending
- Verify you're selecting from the friends list, not all users

**Don't see shared content**
- Check your notifications for sharing confirmations
- Verify the content was shared with you (not just mentioned)
- Refresh your dashboard or collections page

**Lost access to shared content**
- The owner may have removed your access
- Check notifications for unsharing messages
- The owner may have deleted the content entirely

**Can't modify sharing settings**
- Only owners can manage sharing settings
- If you received shared content, contact the owner to request changes
